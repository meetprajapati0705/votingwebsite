Online Voting System

What is Voting?

Voting is the process by which citizens choose leaders or make decisions on important issues. It is the foundation of a democracy, giving people the power to shape the future of their nation. Every vote matters and contributes to the outcome.

Why Voting is Important?

Voting ensures that the voices of the people are heard. It allows everyone to participate in the decision-making process and choose leaders who represent their interests. By voting, you help build a better future for yourself and your community.

Common Questions About Voting

1. Who can vote?

Any citizen aged 18 or above with a valid Voter ID can vote.

2. Is voting mandatory?

No, but it is a responsibility of every citizen.

3. How often do elections happen?

It depends on the position, but general elections happen every 5 years.

4. Can I vote online?

Currently, voting is usually done at polling stations, but technology may change this in the future.

5. What if I don't vote?

Your voice will not be part of the decision-making process.

6. How is my vote counted?

Votes are counted securely by the election commission using EVMs or ballots.

7. Can I change my vote?

No, once submitted, your vote is final.

8. How can I register to vote?

You can register through the official election commission website or voter centers.

9. What is a NOTA option?

NOTA means "None of the Above" if you do not like any candidate.

10. Why should I vote?

Because every single vote shapes the future of the country.
