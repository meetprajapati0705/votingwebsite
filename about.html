About Voting

Voting is the process by which citizens express their choice for leaders or policies. It is a key pillar of democracy, ensuring that the government reflects the will of the people. In India, every citizen aged 18 or above has the legal right to vote, regardless of gender, religion, caste, or economic status.

Who can vote? All Indian citizens who are 18 years or older, mentally sound, not convicted of certain crimes, and registered in the electoral roll are eligible to vote.

Voting helps decide who will represent you in government and what decisions will shape the future of your community and country.

In India, voting is conducted through the Election Commission using secure systems like the Electronic Voting Machine (EVM).

Rules of Voting

  • Only Indian citizens aged 18 years or above are eligible to vote.
  • Voters must be registered in the official electoral roll.
  • Each voter is allowed to cast only one vote.
  • Voters must carry a valid Voter ID or other government-approved ID.
  • Bribery, impersonation, or coercion during voting is strictly prohibited.
  • Voting is confidential — voters must not disclose their choice.
  • Damaging or misusing Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) is a punishable offense.
  • Any kind of campaigning within 100 meters of the polling booth is banned.
  • Discipline and orderly conduct must be maintained inside polling stations.
